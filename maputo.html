Maputo is the capital city of Mozambique and one of the largest urban centres of the country. It is located in the Southern region of Mozambique.

Eduardo Mondlane University, through its Research Unit Center of Diversity, Gender and Health at the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, is devoted to research and advocacy on modern slavery issues, such as sexual exploitation, child marriage, child labour, domestic servitude, human trafficking among others. Multiple partners from Civil Society, UN agencies and private sectors share the objective of making Eduardo Mondlane Univerticy free from modern slavery and towards justice and human rights. Research activities of this Unity include community extension activities and the establishment of forums and spaces enabling debate, counselling and referrals. In addition, awareness campaigns including group activities, sensitization/counselling services, posters, graffiti and wall murals in schools and public streets has been taken in place in several cities including Maputo City.

Key areas of interest:

  • Domestic servitude
  • Albinism
  • Child labour
  • Early marriage
  • Human trafficking
  • Sex workers

Hopes for the project:
To understand factors influencing and reproducing modern slavery in Maputo city. To share experience of how modern slavery manifests in the city, and learn from other cities experience and approaches used to fight against modern slavery.
